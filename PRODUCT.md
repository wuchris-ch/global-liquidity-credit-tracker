# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A single primary user: a macro investor (the project owner) checking conditions each morning before the US open, on a laptop in a bright room, often with a brokerage tab open next to it. Secondary: anyone he shares the link with who wants a credible read on global liquidity without knowing the methodology. Frequency: daily glance, weekly deeper read.

## Product Purpose

Track global liquidity and credit conditions and turn them into one actionable read: what regime are we in (Loose / Neutral / Tight), is it strengthening or fading, what is driving it, where price leadership is showing up (AI, crypto, gold, small caps, duration), and what has that reconstructed regime historically meant for risk assets. The pipeline computes the GLCI (a tri-pillar liquidity/credit/stress factor index), Fed net liquidity, USD credit stress, a liquidity-sensitive price-leadership scoreboard (the Flows page), and a weekly, next-bar backtest of reconstructed regime forward returns. Success: the user gets the regime read in under 30 seconds, sees what would invalidate it, and understands the data limitations before acting on it.

## Brand Personality

Institutional, literate, calm. Three words: written, rigorous, unhurried. The app should feel like a daily research note from a serious shop (Bridgewater Daily Observations, Gavekal), not a SaaS dashboard. It states conclusions in sentences, shows the evidence underneath, and is honest about uncertainty (confidence intervals, sample sizes, data staleness).

## Anti-references

- SaaS dashboard clichés: identical metric-card grids, hero-metric template, gradient accents.
- Bloomberg-terminal pastiche: dense dark screens with neon series colors.
- Crypto-dashboard neon, glassmorphism, decorative motion.
- Generic "fintech navy + gold."

## Design Principles

1. **Verdict first, evidence beneath.** Every page leads with a stated conclusion in prose; charts and tables support it, never substitute for it.
2. **Written, not widgetized.** Prefer a sentence generated from the data over a card with a number. Numbers live inside sentences and tables, set in tabular figures.
3. **Color is meaning.** Regime color (green/amber/red) is the only committed color and appears only where it encodes regime. Everything else is ink on paper.
4. **Honest about uncertainty.** Sample sizes, confidence intervals, and data freshness are part of the content, not footnotes to hide.
5. **The tool disappears into the morning routine.** No onboarding ceremony, no decorative motion, fast static-data loads.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast on the paper theme. Regime states never encoded by color alone (always paired with a label: Loose / Neutral / Tight). Respect prefers-reduced-motion. Charts keep readable axis labels at mobile widths.
